About me

Thank you for visiting my site. I was born in Cuba and live in California. I'm a teacher by profession, with university training in philosophy and education. I'm interested in the intersection of technology and the Humanities, combining my pedagogical vocation with participation in Digital Humanities initiatives. In my free time, I collaborate on citizen projects focused on computational philology, critical editing, history, and open access to digital archives.

I develop digitization and transcription projects for classical works translated into Spanish, such as Tacitus’ Annals (ed. Moralejo, Gredos 1979) and Thucydides’ History of the Peloponnesian War (trans. Torres Esbarranch, Gredos 1990). These involve careful correction and conversion from PDF to markdown. The results are published—perhaps for the first time on the Web—as navigable and reusable editions using tools like Pandoc, Python, Hugo, Markdown, Git, Visual Studio Code, TXM, and more.

Another interest of mine is translating key resources from English into Spanish to broaden access in the Spanish-speaking world. This includes the Text Encoding Initiative (TEI) repository, which is vital for anyone working in the digital preservation of texts. I'm currently studying XML-TEI and translating parts of the TEI Guidelines.

Translation and transcription enrich one’s own learning while contributing to the preservation, portability, and dissemination of classical works and digital resources in Spanish. These practices not only support the spread of knowledge, but also promote open access to culture and education more broadly.
