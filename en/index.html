About Me

I am a teacher by profession, with a university background in philosophy and education. A web technologies enthusiast, I combine my pedagogical vocation with active participation in Digital Humanities initiatives. In my free time, I contribute to citizen-led projects focused on computational philology, critical editing, collaborative history, and open access to digital archives.

One of my main interests is the translation and dissemination of key resources from English into Spanish, in order to broaden their accessibility across the Spanish-speaking world. Among these is the Text Encoding Initiative (TEI) Repository, whose documentation and tools are essential for those working in the digital preservation of texts.

I am currently working on digitizing, transcribing, and publishing classical texts in open formats, including Tacitus’ Annals (ed. Moralejo, Gredos 1979) and Thucydides’ History of the Peloponnesian War (trans. Torres Esbarranch, Gredos 1990). These projects involve careful correction and adaptation from PDF sources, with the goal of preserving fidelity to the original. The texts are being published online for the first time in browsable and reusable formats, using tools such as Pandoc, Python, Hugo, Markdown, and Git.

I also integrate the use of language models such as ChatGPT to automate editorial tasks, support text correction, and explore new possibilities in mediating humanistic knowledge through emerging technologies.
