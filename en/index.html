On Translating

Translation, that is, the task of taking a text from one language to another, is the most transcendental way by which ideas are transmitted and access is facilitated to texts that are naturally foreign to the vast majority of readers. If we all spoke only one language or if we all spoke all the languages, translation would not be necessary. The act of translating, as anyone who has at least tried it knows, requires great cognitive effort. The translator approaches the reading of the original text in a very peculiar way, using techniques and instruments, for example a bilingual dictionary, a lexicon, that facilitates the understanding of the text. Translating, furthermore, requires investigating the author, his ideas and the historical context in which they were developed. Therefore, translation requires, in addition to linguistic skills, science and imagination.
