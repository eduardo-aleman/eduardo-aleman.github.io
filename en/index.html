About Me

I am a teacher by profession, with a university background in philosophy and education. A web technologies enthusiast, I combine my pedagogical vocation with active participation in Digital Humanities initiatives. In my free time, I contribute to citizen-led projects focused on computational philology, critical editing, collaborative history, and open access to digital archives.

One of my interests is the translation and dissemination of key resources from English into Spanish, with the aim of increasing their accessibility in the Spanish-speaking world. Among these resources is the Text Encoding Initiative (TEI) repository, whose documentation and tools are essential for those working in the digital preservation of texts. I recently began translating its Guidelines.

I also develop projects for the digitization, transcription, and publication in open formats of classic works such as the Annals of Tacitus (ed. Moralejo, Gredos 1979) and Thucydides' History of the Peloponnesian War (trans. Torres Esbarranch, Gredos 1990). These projects involve detailed correction and adaptation from PDF, with the aim of preserving fidelity to the original text. The results are published for the first time on the web as browsable and reusable editions, using tools such as Pandoc, Python, Hugo, Markdown, and Git.
