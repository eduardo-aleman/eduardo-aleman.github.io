About

I am a school teacher by profession, with academic training in philosophy and education. As a citizen initiative in my spare time, I contribute to digital humanities projects focused on computational philology, critical editions, collaborative history, and public access to digital archives. I also translate code resources from English to Spanish to expand their accessibility, including the Text Encoding Initiative Repository.

My current projects include the digital publication of classical texts such as Tacitus’ Annals (trans. José Luis Moralejo, Gredos 1979) and Thucydides’ History of the Peloponnesian War (trans. Juan José Torres Esbarranch, Gredos 1990). These involve careful transcription and correction of PDF-converted texts to preserve their integrity. The works are published online —for the first time, to our knowledge— in open formats for navigation and academic reuse, using tools like Pandoc, Python, Hugo, Markdown, and Git.
