Anaphora

An anaphora is a rhetorical figure that consists of the deliberate repetition of a word or phrase at the beginning of several consecutive clauses, sentences, or verses. This repetition has the effect of emphasizing an idea, creating a rhythm, or highlighting an emotion. Anaphora is a powerful tool in poetry, prose, and speech to capture the reader's or listener's attention and to underline a specific theme or feeling.
